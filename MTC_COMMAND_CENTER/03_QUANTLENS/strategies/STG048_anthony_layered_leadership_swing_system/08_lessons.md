# Lessons — Anthony swing trader interview (`_QewlGLBaeA`)

Trader wisdom worth keeping even beyond the mechanical system.

- **Layered margin of safety**: stack independent edges (RS + theme + environment +
  pattern). Conviction/size scales with how many layers align.
- **Math of the game**: expectancy = win% × avg win − loss% × avg loss. A ~30% win
  rate wins if winners are big and losers are cut at the low of day. Never let a
  stock go to zero (it breaks the win/loss math).
- **Three levels of skill**: (1) memorize patterns, (2) see sectors/themes/rotation,
  (3) read the market environment/cycle. Emotional control sits on top.
- **Situational awareness > pattern purity**: in a strong leader/theme the exact
  pattern matters less; in a weak name the pattern must be perfect.
- **Cycle timing**: early-cycle breakouts run straight (don't wait for a retest);
  late-cycle favors pullback entries. Press the gas early, cut exposure in chop.
- **Risk first**: simplest workable rule — stop at low of day, then trail. You can
  sleep at night because risk is predefined.
- **Process/routine**: daily "backwatch" list of biggest movers → Mon-Fri template →
  reassess each morning; breadth of breakouts is a market-health gauge.
- **Don't fight your own read**: trade what the market is actually doing, not what you
  think it "should" do (no "is it faking me out"). 
- **Deliberate practice**: setups happen only a few times a year; you can't shortcut
  screen time. Deep-dive one mentor's process fully before adding others.

## Philosophy
- **Weekly charts only** for decisions → patience, fewer/better trades, less
  impulsivity. "I stopped trading so much and profits skyrocketed."
- **Risk/reward commandments**: only take trades with large R:R asymmetry; on a
  weekly chart a 100% move isn't rare, so time is on your side.

## Selection / entry setups
- **10W_30W_RESPECT_ENTRY** — price respects rising 10-week / 30-week MAs → long.
- **WEEKLY_CHARACTER_CHANGE / TREND_STARTER** — the core setup: weekly price action
  shifts character (downtrend → first higher low/close strength) = trend start.
- **WEEKLY_BASE_TIGHTNESS_ACCUMULATION** — tight weekly base = quiet accumulation.
- **UP_ON_VOLUME_RADAR / UNIVERSE_SCAN** — scan for up-on-volume weekly leaders.
- **CONTROLLED_WEAKNESS_BASE_RETEST** — shallow, controlled pullback retest of base.

## Risk / sizing / exit
- **WEEKLY_CLOSE_STOP_POLICY** — stops trigger on **weekly closes only** (ignore
  intraweek noise).
- **ASYMMETRIC_RISK_REWARD_FILTER** — skip trades without big upside vs risk.
- **EARNINGS_CUSHION_RULE** — only hold into earnings with a profit cushion.
- **TRADE_FREQUENCY_GUARD** — cap trade frequency (weekly cadence) to avoid overtrading.
- **WEEKLY_SCALE_IN_ON_PROOF** — add only after the weekly bar proves the move.
- **WEEKLY_SELL_DECISION_FRAMEWORK** — structured weekly sell rules (the most-requested
  topic; sell on character change down / loss of 10W).

## Reusable components for MTC
- Weekly resampling, 10W/30W MA respect gate, character-change detector, up-on-volume
  scan, weekly-base tightness, weekly-close stop engine, asymmetric-R:R filter,
  earnings-cushion guard, trade-frequency throttle, scale-in-on-proof.

## Lessons (keep)
- Slower timeframe = fewer decisions = less self-sabotage. Trade less, win more.
- Stops on closes (not intraweek wicks) avoid noise shake-outs.
- Demand asymmetric R:R; don't compare your stage to others'.

## Risks for backtesting
- Few signals (weekly) → small sample; test long histories.
- "Character change" needs an objective trigger to be testable.

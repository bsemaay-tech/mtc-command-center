## Style
Swing / trend-follower who moved from breakouts (got shaken out) to **pullbacks**,
trades **both long and short**, learned pullbacks from the short side first.

## The 3 setups
1. **PARABOLIC_LONG** — after a parabolic **down** extension (e.g. price ~**20% below
   the 1-hour EMA**), buy the **snapback** reversal. (Mirror: parabolic short on
   over-extension up.)
2. **PULLBACK_CONFLUENCE_LONG** — in an uptrend, buy the pullback into a **confluence**
   support (EMA + level), entering on the reversal.
3. **PULLBACK_SHORT** — in a downtrend, short the **counter-rally** into resistance.

## Entry / risk / exit
- **Entry:** extension % from 1h EMA (parabolic) or confluence pullback + reversal bar.
  Best entries are tight: gappers up at the open often get slammed and take out the
  low of day, then **set up again later in the day** — wait for the re-setup.
- **Stop:** at/under the reversal low (long) ; strict — "even with 50% drawdowns I just
  keep getting stopped out" (small, frequent stops).
- **Exit:** trail the whole position up; **sell into strength** when extended on the
  lower timeframe.

## Reusable components for MTC
- Parabolic-extension detector (% distance from 1h EMA), confluence-pullback entry,
  short-side pullback engine, sell-into-strength trim, full-position trail.

## Lessons (keep)
- Pullbacks > raw breakouts for shake-out-prone names; learn shorts to understand
  pullbacks.
- Quantify "parabolic" objectively (% from a reference EMA) instead of eyeballing.
- Early gappers often fail then re-set later in the session — patience on entry.

## Risks for backtesting
- Parabolic reversals are counter-trend (timing/knife-catch risk).
- A single +969% year is likely regime-specific — test across regimes; expect lower.

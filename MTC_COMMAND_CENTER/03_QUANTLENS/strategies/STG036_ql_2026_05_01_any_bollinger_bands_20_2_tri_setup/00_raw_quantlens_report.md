## Transcript-Derived Summary
The transcript explains Bollinger Bands as a volatility indicator using the standard `20` period simple moving average and `2` standard deviation bands. Narrow bands represent low-volatility range conditions. Wide bands represent high-volatility trending or momentum conditions.

Three setup families are described:

1. Breakout: identify a sideways market with narrow bands and price mostly contained inside the bands, then enter when a strong candle breaks and closes outside the band/range.
2. Reversal: in sideways conditions, fade upper-band touches for shorts or lower-band touches for longs, targeting movement back through the range.
3. Pullback: in an uptrend where price trades between the middle and upper band, buy pullbacks to the middle band; in a downtrend where price trades between the middle and lower band, sell pullbacks to the middle band.

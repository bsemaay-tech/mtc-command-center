## Intake-stated core thesis
Kell teaches a 12-stage **cycle of price action**: reversal extension → snapback to 10/20EMA → pullback / higher low → contraction / mini-base → wedge pop → EMA crossback → basin break → exhaustion extension → lower high → wedge drop → downtrend.

Trading edge is selecting the right phase of the cycle on the right stock (a true leader in an in-play theme), then entering on a structurally tight setup, *not* on a generic moving-average crossover.

## Distinct setups identified
1. **Wedge Pop** — after capitulation+snapback+pullback+contraction with higher low, break of mini-base swing high. (Primary entry setup.)
2. **EMA Crossback** — after a wedge pop, pullback into the 10/20EMA zone, lower-TF higher low forms, break of local swing high.
3. **Basin Break** — 1–3 week consolidation back into MAs *after* trend already started.
4. **Exhaustion Extension / Wedge Drop** — exit / short-side; price extended → bounce → lower high → MA loss.

## Multi-timeframe rule (intake quote, paraphrased)
- Trades are *not created* on the lower timeframe. Weekly/daily structure must drive the idea; lower TF is execution only.

## Universe rule
- Strongest stocks; in-play themes; liquid high-beta names; names ahead of indexes in cycle (forming higher lows while index forms lower lows).

## Position sizing (Kell's reported)
- Top liquid leaders: 30–35% per name.
- Liquid core: 15–20%.
- Volatile lower-priced: ~7%; volatile higher-priced: ~12%.

## Intake's own warning
- "Tight mini base" and "extension" need objective thresholds; remain partially discretionary.
- 65-minute is equity-session-specific; crypto adaptation must use 1h/4h.
- Best results depend heavily on stock selection / themes / high-beta leaders — *crypto BTC/ETH/SOL backtest cannot prove this edge*.
